Arthoscopic repair

This minimally invasive procedure uses a small camera and surgical instruments inserted through tiny incisions to diagnose and treat joint problems like torn cartilage or loose bone fragments.

Joint Replacement

This involves replacing a damaged joint (like the hip or knee) with an artificial one

Fracture Repair

Broken bones are fixed with screws, plates, rods, or pins to promote healing in the correct position.

Ligament and Tendon Repair

Torn ligaments or tendons can be repaired surgically to restore stability and function.

Spine Surgery

This can involve procedures like disc replacement, spinal fusion, or decompression surgery to relieve pain and improve function in the spine.

Cartilage Repair

Damaged cartilage can be repaired or replaced using techniques like microfracture or cartilage transplantation.

Bone Grafting

Bone grafts are used to fill in bone defects or promote bone healing.

Amputation

In severe cases, amputation may be necessary to remove a limb that is severely injured or diseased.
