Refraction testing

This is a simple eye exam that measures how well your eyes focus light. It helps determine if you need glasses or contact lenses to correct your vision.

dry eye screening

This is a simple test that evaluates the health of your eyes and tears. It helps identify conditions like dry eye syndrome, which can cause discomfort, blurred vision, and eye irritation.

vitamin A deficiency screening in children

This is a test to check for signs of vitamin A deficiency, such as night blindness and dry eyes. Early detection and treatment can prevent serious health problems like blindness.

uveitis screening in autoimmune diseases

This is a test to check for inflammation in the eye, a condition known as uveitis. It's particularly important for people with autoimmune diseases, as they are at increased risk of developing uveitis. Early detection and treatment can prevent vision loss.

Glaucoma screening

This is a test to check for inflammation in the eye, a condition that can increase the risk of glaucoma. Early detection and treatment of uveitis can help prevent vision loss associated with glaucoma.

colour vision and binocular vision testing

Color vision testing assesses your ability to distinguish between different colors. Binocular vision testing evaluates how well your two eyes work together to focus and perceive depth.

macular function tests

Macular function tests assess the health of the macula, a small area in the center of the retina responsible for sharp, detailed vision. These tests can help detect early signs of macular degeneration and other eye conditions that affect central vision.

keratoconus screening

Keratoconus screening is a test to check for a progressive eye condition where the cornea thins and bulges outward, affecting vision. Early detection and management can help prevent vision loss.

diabetic retinopathy screening

Diabetic retinopathy screening is a test to check for damage to the blood vessels in the retina caused by diabetes. Early detection and treatment can help prevent vision loss.

hypertensive retinopathy screening

Hypertensive retinopathy screening is a test to check for damage to the blood vessels in the retina caused by high blood pressure. Early detection and management can help prevent vision loss.

High myopia retinal examination

High myopia retinal examination is a specialized eye exam for individuals with high nearsightedness. It helps detect early signs of eye problems, such as retinal detachment, glaucoma, and cataracts, which are more common in people with high myopia.

ARMD ( Age related macular degeneration screening ) in elderly

Age-related macular degeneration (AMD) screening is a test to check for damage to the macula, a small area in the center of the retina. Early detection of AMD can help slow its progression and preserve vision.

Upper GI endoscopy

Upper GI endoscopy is a procedure that uses a flexible tube with a camera (endoscope) to examine the upper digestive tract, including the esophagus, stomach, and duodenum. It helps diagnose conditions like ulcers, inflammation, and cancer.

Ultrasound abdomen

An ultrasound abdomen is a non-invasive imaging test that uses sound waves to create pictures of your internal organs. It helps diagnose conditions affecting the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, kidneys, and other abdominal organs.

Proctoscopy

Proctoscopy is a medical procedure that involves using a proctoscope, a short, rigid or flexible tube with a light source, to examine the lower part of the rectum and anus. It helps diagnose conditions like hemorrhoids, polyps, and inflammation.

USG guided FNAC of lumps

USG-guided FNAC (Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology) is a minimally invasive procedure that uses ultrasound to guide a thin needle to collect cells from a lump or mass. These cells are then examined under a microscope to determine if the lump is cancerous or benign.

Infertility treatment

Infertility treatment aims to help couples conceive a child. It involves various methods like medication to stimulate ovulation, surgical procedures to correct reproductive issues, and assisted reproductive technologies (ART) such as In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) and Intrauterine Insemination (IUI).

Evaluation and treatment for dysfunctional uterine bleeding

Evaluation and treatment for dysfunctional uterine bleeding (DUB) involve assessing the underlying cause, such as hormonal imbalances or structural issues. Treatment options may include hormonal therapy, medication to regulate bleeding, or surgical procedures in severe cases.

Polycystic ovarian disease treatment (PCOD)

Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) treatment aims to manage symptoms like irregular periods, acne, and excess hair growth. Treatment options may include lifestyle changes like weight loss and regular exercise, as well as medications to regulate hormones and improve fertility.

Family planning services

Family planning services provide information and methods to help individuals and couples plan and space their pregnancies. These services include contraception, fertility awareness methods, and counseling on reproductive health.

VIA – VILLI ( screening for cervical cancer )

VIA-VILI (Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid and Lugol's Iodine) is a simple screening test for cervical cancer. It involves applying acetic acid and Lugol's iodine to the cervix to identify abnormal cells that may indicate precancerous or cancerous changes.

Fractional curettage / Hysteroscopy

Fractional curettage and hysteroscopy are minimally invasive procedures used to diagnose and treat conditions affecting the uterus. Curettage involves removing tissue from the lining of the uterus, while hysteroscopy allows for direct visualization of the uterine cavity using a small camera.

Total lap hysterectomy

Total laparoscopic hysterectomy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure to remove the uterus through small incisions in the abdomen. It's often used to treat conditions like heavy menstrual bleeding, uterine fibroids, and endometriosis.

Lap myomectomy

Laparoscopic myomectomy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used to remove fibroids from the uterus. It involves making small incisions in the abdomen and using specialized instruments to access and remove the fibroids. This technique offers faster recovery times and less pain compared to traditional open surgery.

Surgical oncology

Surgical oncology is a branch of medicine that focuses on the surgical treatment of cancer. Surgeons specializing in this field remove cancerous tumors and surrounding tissue, often in combination with other treatments like chemotherapy and radiation therapy.

Urology

Urology is the branch of medicine that focuses on the urinary tract and male reproductive system. Urologists diagnose and treat conditions affecting the kidneys, bladder, ureters, urethra, and male reproductive organs.

Surgical Gastroenterology

Surgical gastroenterology is a specialized field of medicine that focuses on the surgical treatment of diseases affecting the digestive system. Surgeons in this field perform operations on organs like the stomach, intestines, liver, gallbladder, and pancreas to treat conditions such as cancer, ulcers, and inflammatory bowel disease.

Plastic surgery

Plastic surgery is a surgical specialty focused on restoring, reconstructing, or altering the human body. It encompasses a wide range of procedures, from reconstructive surgery to cosmetic procedures like facelifts and breast augmentation.

Psychiatry

Psychiatry is a branch of medicine that deals with the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of mental, emotional, and behavioral disorders. Psychiatrists are medical doctors who can prescribe medication and provide therapy to help patients manage their mental health conditions.
